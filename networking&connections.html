Networking & Connections

Building Your Professional Network

Networking is not just about asking for jobs — it’s about cultivating genuine relationships that support your career growth. UMSI students can benefit from connecting with peers, alumni, and professionals in information fields to learn about different roles, industries, and opportunities.

Strategies for Effective Networking

  1. Attend Events: Career fairs, workshops, and guest lectures give you face-to-face opportunities with employers and alumni.
  2. Leverage LinkedIn: Build a complete profile, join industry groups, and reach out to alumni using the U-M alumni tool.
  3. Use Informational Interviews: Ask professionals about their career path, work environment, and advice. The TIARA method makes this simple.
  4. Stay in Touch: Networking is ongoing — send thank-you notes, share updates, and maintain your connections over time.

Tips for Students New to Networking

  • Start small: connect with classmates and faculty first.
  • Practice your “elevator pitch” — a 30-second intro about your interests and skills.
  • Set a monthly networking goal, like attending one event or reaching out to two alumni.
